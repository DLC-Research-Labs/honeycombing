# COI Registry Schema (Objective 4)

Date: 2026-07-03
Status: registry + fracture gate + Communities panel shipped with one gated FICTIONAL sample payload; no real COI ingested yet.

## Purpose

Communities of interest are the human counterweight to every geometric layer in Honeycombing. A COI is somebody's
claim that "these people belong together" — shared schools, transit, industry, language, history. The tool's job is
to show where district lines cut through that claim, never to decide whether the claim is valid and never to fold it
into a score. A fractured COI is a review question, not a violation: the fracture may be a lawful VRA adjustment, a
county-line consequence, or a reviewable choice, and Honeycombing cannot tell those apart.

## Where the schema lives

- Types + helpers: `app/lib/honeycomb-ui-helpers.ts` (`CoiSummary`, `CoiRegistry`, `getCoiRegistryUrl`, `getCoiFractureGate`, `COI_OVERLAY_COLOR`)
- Registry: `public/data/cois/registry.json`
- Sample payload: `public/data/cois/nc-sample-fictional-crescent.json`, generated by `scripts/build-sample-coi.mjs` (deterministic; fracture recomputed from tracked artifacts, byte-stable)

## Shape

A `CoiSummary` carries four things, in order of importance:

1. **Provenance** — `submitter` (name, type: individual/organization/portal/synthetic) and `source` (description,
   collection date, drawing method, url). This is not metadata; a COI without a submitter of record is not
   reviewable. The UI shows provenance before any polygon renders.
2. **`narrative`** — the community's own story, written by the submitter. Displayed verbatim, never scored.
3. **`geometry`** — one GeoJSON Feature (polygon).
4. **`fracture`** — precomputed district-touch counts per registered plan: H3 cells whose centers fall inside the
   polygon, population-weighted segments per district, `districtsTouched`, and `largestShare` (population cohesion).
   Always claim-tagged (`descriptive_with_assignment_caveat` while cell-center assignment is the method).

## The fracture gate

`getCoiFractureGate(coi)` blocks fracture display when:

- `status === "sample"` or `submitter.type === "synthetic"` — fictional geometry must never read as a community;
- submitter name is missing — no accountable origin;
- `source.description` or `source.collectedAt` is missing — collection provenance cannot be audited.

Gated COIs render dashed at reduced opacity with fracture numbers marked "preview", exactly like the ensemble
outlier gate. Enabling the layer always opens the Communities panel first so provenance is on screen before
polygons (same principle as constraints-before-bands).

## What the fracture metric is and is not

- Population-based: share of COI population per district under each plan. Deliberately NOT partisan — mixing the
  vote proxy into a COI readout would turn a community claim into a partisan instrument.
- `largestShare` is the cohesion headline: 1.0 = the community sits whole in one district.
- Comparing plans (e.g. largest share 89% under the court plan vs 62% under the 2023 enacted plan for the sample)
  is the entire product: which plan keeps the claimed community together.
- Cell-center assignment; real reviewer-facing fracture numbers need polygon apportionment (see centroid audits).

## Open questions for expert review

- Is population share the right fracture measure, or should it be paired with a perimeter/boundary-length measure of
  how the cut runs through the community?
- When a COI overlaps a VRA-adjacent region (e.g. the Eastern Black Belt), how should the display distinguish
  "fractured by a possibly-required VRA configuration" from "fractured by a reviewable choice" without implying either?
- Should multiple overlapping COI submissions for the same area render as a heat count (Districtr-style) or stay
  strictly individual with their own provenance?
